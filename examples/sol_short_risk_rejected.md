## Interpretation

This example demonstrates a critical feature: **a technically valid setup that gets rejected by the risk layer.**

The technical analysis is clean. Four of five layers agree on a short setup. The downtrend is structurally confirmed (EMA alignment), volatility is expanding in the bearish direction, MACD is accelerating down, and RSI is fading from overbought territory. The FVG layer didn't reach agreement (42% sell, below the 50% threshold) because the detected gap is 12 bars old and approaching the staleness cutoff.

But the conservative risk profile raises two flags:

1. **Position sizing**: The calculated size of 18.3% far exceeds the conservative cap of 5%. The risk layer hard-caps it down.
2. **Risk/reward ratio**: The 1:2.0 ratio doesn't meet the conservative minimum of 1:2.5.

The result: **FLAGGED**. Under the conservative profile, this trade doesn't pass. A trader using the moderate or aggressive profile would see it approved (moderate cap is 15%, moderate min R/R is 1:2.0).

This is the kind of nuance that separates a research framework from a signal service. The same setup can be valid for one risk tolerance and invalid for another. The system makes that distinction explicit rather than pretending one-size-fits-all.

**What a trader would do here:** Either switch to the moderate profile (if their risk tolerance genuinely supports it), or wait for a higher-conviction entry with a tighter stop that improves the R/R ratio above 1:2.5. The framework gives them the information to make that decision themselves.
